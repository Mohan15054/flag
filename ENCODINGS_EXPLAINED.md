## 📚 What is This Monument About?

This digital artwork is a living tribute to **India's Republic Day** — January 26th, when India's Constitution came into force in 1950. Every visual element you see **encodes an important constitutional number or concept**. It's like a puzzle where the numbers ARE the design.

**What it means:** The year India's Constitution came into force  
**Where you see it:** In the **shape and proportions** of the entire artwork  
**How it works:** The main rectangle has a width-to-height ratio of **19.5 to 10** (which equals 1950 when multiplied by 100)  
**Why it matters:** This is the birth year of the Indian Republic

## 🔄 HOW THE ANIMATIONS WORK

1. **Pulsing Rings:** The three main rings grow and shrink, like a heartbeat — showing that the Constitution is alive
2. **Rotating Monument:** Everything rotates slowly (75 seconds) — representing the passage of time

5. **Floating Particles:** Randomly moving dots in the background — representing the people participating in democracy

## 💡 WHY IS THIS MONUMENT IMPORTANT?

### **It's NOT a Flag**
This is NOT India's flag. It's a different kind of tribute — one that celebrates the **invisible** but powerful Constitution that holds India together.

### **Every Number Has Meaning**
Unlike decorative art, **every element here encodes something real about India's democratic system**. Change any number, and the design breaks — showing how interconnected all these constitutional elements are.

### **It's a Living Monument**
Unlike a statue or painting, this monument moves and changes. It's alive — just like the Constitution, which is constantly being applied, interpreted, and renewed by India's courts and people.

The monument uses:
